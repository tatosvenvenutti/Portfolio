Knight AI

While our game originally only had zombies, I took the leap and told my team I would take a swing at AI. I never looked back; creating these knights was something I was so proud of, from designing their attacks to developing behavior trees and debugging through the knights. They brought me joy like no other. With these knights, I wanted them to have combos, charge attacks, and blocking followed by a counter-attack. I am so glad I was able to make it all happen with what I had the knowledge of.

  • Designed and developed actors and behavior trees
  • Developed blocking, combos, charge attacks, patrolling features in C++
  • Developed customizable weapons, allowing each enemy to have different weapons

Boss AI

While not my most ambitious project, this was the first enemy I developed, and a boss no less. I wanted him to be more defensive and almost territorial, where he shoots projectiles to make you push him and then use large sweeping attacks to make you keep your distance. This created an almost back-and-forth rhythm of retreating and advancing.

  • Designed and developed actor and behavior tree
  • Developed area-of-effect attacks, projectiles feature in C++

Non-Hostile AI

To add more life into the open world, nature must be present. So, as a last-minute decision, I programmed basic animal AI for deer (shown in the GIF), pigs, wolves, and foxes. These were something I never intended on making, but nevertheless, it was something I wanted to give a shot.

  • Designed and developed actor and behavior tree
  • Wanted to bring life into the world

References and Inspirations

With the help of my fantastic team, our goal was to create a large world like Elden Ring, with the combo strings of Monster Hunter and the fluidity of Vindictus.

Audio Design

Audio is a crucial part of video games, and I wanted it to be not only noticeable but also subtle. I created different audio cues for character movements based on the roughness and materials. One example is soft steps provided.

  • Finding royalty-free songs and audio
  • Inserted audio effects, ambiance, and themes
  • Implemented a dynamic footstep system via blueprints

Minor Level Design

Even though I'm not a level designer, I took on the task of creating an eerie boss room for the second boss battle. I added bones and cages rattling around, and designed the boss to drop from the ceiling. Additionally, I created patrolling paths for added challenge.

Minor UI Desing

Even though UI design wasn't my primary role in this project, I assisted my team by designing the boss UI and programming a stun meter into the game.

  • Designed the general Boss UI
  • Programmed the stun component and implemented the UI stun meter
